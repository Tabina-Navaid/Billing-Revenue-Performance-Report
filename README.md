# Billing-Revenue-Performance-Report
Financial dashboard built for a German company, tracking collections and revenue across a 5-year period. 
Project Description
A multi-tab Power BI financial dashboard built for a German company, tracking billing, collections, and revenue across a 5-year period. Connected to a live Microsoft Azure data pipeline, the report gives finance and operations teams a single source of truth for monitoring €576K+ in total revenue, €234K in billed amounts, and €117K in extracted (collected) money — with dynamic filters across weekly, monthly, and yearly views.


Project Overview
Problem Statement
The company lacked a unified view of its financial performance across billing, collections, and revenue. Data was siloed in Azure, with no easy way for stakeholders to track how much had been invoiced versus actually collected, identify peak-performing periods, or monitor partner-level extraction trends — making it difficult to spot cash flow gaps or revenue anomalies in time to act.

Approach
A three-tab Power BI report was developed on top of an Azure data pipeline, each tab purpose-built for a distinct financial lens. The Billed Amount tab tracks total invoiced amounts with year-over-year breakdowns and cumulative growth trends. The Extracted Money tab monitors collections by partner (BML vs. MB), exposing how much of what was billed was actually recovered. The Revenue Report tab provides the full income picture with monthly, weekly, and annual comparisons. Dynamic slicers allow stakeholders to slice any metric by custom date ranges, making the report usable at every level of the business.

Business Outcome
The dashboard revealed that 2021 was the strongest year in both billed (€78K) and extracted money (€5K peak in March 2023), while also showing a visible downward trend in monthly billed amounts through early 2026 — an early warning signal for the finance team. By comparing billed vs. extracted figures (€234K billed vs. €117K extracted), the business could now quantify its collection gap and take targeted action to improve recovery rates, directly supporting cash flow management and partner accountability.
